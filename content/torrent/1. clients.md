Best BitTorrent clients for downloading files.

## Safety Warning

> [!DANGER]
> **BIND YOUR VPN INTERFACE.**
> Just running a VPN is not enough. If your VPN connection drops for a second, your real IP address will leak to the torrent swarm (and copyright trolls).
>
> **How to fix in qBittorrent:**
> 1.  Open Settings > Advanced.
> 2.  Find "Network Interface".
> 3.  Change it from "Any interface" to your VPN adapter (e.g., `wg0`, `tun0`, `Mullvad`).
> 4.  Now, if the VPN disconnects, qBittorrent **cannot** access the internet. It acts as a foolproof Kill Switch.

## Desktop (Windows / Linux / macOS)

### [qBittorrent](https://www.qbittorrent.org/)
The gold standard.
*   **Why use it**:
    *   **Open Source**: No hidden miners or malware (unlike uTorrent/BitTorrent).
    *   **Feature Rich**: Search engine support, RSS feeds, Sequential downloading (watch while downloading), and IP filtering.
    *   **No Ads**: Clean interface.

### [Transmission](https://transmissionbt.com/)
Extremely lightweight and simple client.
*   **Why use it**: Minimal interface. It runs as a daemon on servers (Headless) or as a native app on macOS/Linux. It just works.

### [PicoTorrent](https://picotorrent.org/)
A tiny, high-performance client for Windows.
*   **Why use it**: Built using native Windows APIs (no Electon/Qt heavy frameworks). It starts instantly and uses negligible RAM.

## Android

### [LibreTorrent](https://gitlab.com/proninyaroslav/libretorrent)
The best FOSS client regarding design and features.
*   **Why use it**: Material Design 2.0 interface. Supports Tor, proxy, and scheduling. No ads.

### [Flud](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.delphicoder.flud)
(Closed Source) A solid alternative.
*   **Why use it**: If LibreTorrent fails or you prefer the UI.
*   *Warning*: The free version shows ads.
